Why Women Struggle More

Women’s bodies are biologically wired to store fat more efficiently due to evolutionary and hormonal differences. Hormonal fluctuations throughout the menstrual cycle, pregnancy, and menopause influence hunger and satiety signals, making it more challenging to maintain a caloric deficit consistently.

Dr. Seojin explains, "Estrogen and progesterone can influence the production of ghrelin and leptin, the hormones responsible for hunger and satiety. During certain phases of the menstrual cycle, women are more likely to experience intense cravings and increased appetite."
